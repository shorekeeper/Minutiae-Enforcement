MEMinutiae Enforcement docs

Commands

Reference for the sanction issuance grammar, the lift family, appeals, and diagnostics. Permission nodes are listed per form; see Permissions for bundles.

Issuing sanctions

/enforce <player> [::layout] [@annotation...] [key=value...]

Requires minutiae.command.enforce. The specification is a free-order token stream; every token is one of four kinds:

::key Selects a configured layout, which supplies the rule, measure, duration, escalation ladder, and default annotations. At most one per command.
@name(...) An annotation: a behavioural flag or a directive. Parameters go in parentheses; !@name removes a layout-supplied annotation.
key=value A scalar override. Recognised keys are duration and reason. Quote values containing spaces: reason="two words".
anything else The target player. Exactly one is required.
Note

A sanction needs a measure from exactly one source: a layout, @measure(...), or @remand. A layout's measure is changed with @commute(...), never with @measure.

Examples

# Layout with its configured rule, duration, and escalation:
/enforce Steve ::griefing

# Manual sanction; temporal measures require a duration:
/enforce Steve @measure(MUTE) duration=3h reason="Flooding chat"

# Downgrade a layout's measure, citing an extra rule:
/enforce Steve ::cheating @commute(SUSPENSION) duration=30d @count(P.4.1)

# Preview without applying anything:
/enforce Steve ::toxic @dry-run

Lifting sanctions

/enforce lift <id> [vacate|pardon|served] [@annotation...] [reason...]

Requires minutiae.command.lift. The kind decides what the lifted record continues to mean; see Lifting sanctions for the full model.

vacate The sanction was wrongly issued. The record leaves precedent entirely. This is the default when no kind is given.
pardon Rightly issued, remitted as clemency. Precedent is kept. Requires minutiae.lift.pardon.
served Terminated early as fulfilled. Precedent is kept. Requires minutiae.lift.served.

Bare tokens after the kind form the reason. Admissible annotations:

@silentSuppress the public lift announcement.
@dry-runPreview the lift, including cascade extent.
@note(text)Attach an internal case note.
@notify(ch...)Route a lift notification to channels.
@probation(1w) Open a probation window on the lifted record. Incompatible with vacate, whose record leaves precedent.
@cascade Extend the lift over the whole joinder chain. Requires minutiae.lift.cascade.
Permissions

Two gates depend on the target: lifting another staff member's sanction requires minutiae.lift.foreign, and lifting a ban requires minutiae.lift.blocking. Both apply to every entry path, including appeal acceptance.

Reversing a lift

/enforce unlift <id> [reason...]

Requires minutiae.lift.reinstate. Restores a lifted sanction: an unexpired ban removes the subject from the server, and behavioural constraints re-apply immediately.

Bulk lift

/enforce liftall rule <id> [confirm [reason...]]
/enforce liftall staff <name> [confirm [reason...]]

Requires minutiae.lift.bulk. Without confirm the command only reports how many sanctions match. Bulk lifts are always vacaturs.

Inspection

/enforce info <id> Full view of one sanction: status, kind if lifted, remaining duration, issuer.
/enforce docket <player> [page] A player's complete sanction history.
/enforce appeals [page] Appeals awaiting review.
/enforce appeal <id> accept|deny [reason] Decide an appeal. Acceptance vacates the sanction.

Player appeals

/appeal <sanction-id> <text>

Available to every player by default. A sanction can be appealed once while pending; identifiers tab-complete from the player's own recent sanctions.

Diagnostics

/me-ban exposes subsystem state: ping (storage probe), rules, layouts, annotations, fingerprint <player>, verbose on|off, and reload. Each subcommand carries its own node under minutiae.command.*.
