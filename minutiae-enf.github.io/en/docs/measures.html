Measures

A measure is the enforcement mechanism of a sanction. There are seven, in a fixed severity order, each with a temporal class that decides whether it takes a duration.

The seven measures

MeasureSeverityTemporal class Effect
WARN0 instantaneous A formal warning delivered to the player and recorded against the rule's warning count.
CENSURE1 instantaneous A recorded reprimand: like a warning in delivery, but counted as real precedent, not as a warning.
MUTE2 temporal Chat and configured commands are suppressed; the player is told when they try.
KICK3 instantaneous Immediate removal from the server. No lasting session effect.
QUARANTINE4 temporal Confinement within the configured anchor radius. Also the mechanism behind @remand.
SUSPENSION5 temporal A temporary ban: connection refused until expiry.
CUSTODY6 permanent A permanent ban.

Temporal classes

The class is enforced, not advisory - a violation is a validation error at layout load or command resolve:

instantaneous WARN, CENSURE, KICK. The measure resolves at the moment of application; declaring a duration is an error.
temporal MUTE, QUARANTINE, SUSPENSION. A finite duration is mandatory - from the layout, the escalation ladder, or a duration= override.
permanent CUSTODY. The duration is fixed to permanent; a declared one is ignored.
# Error: KICK takes no duration
/enforce Steve @measure(KICK) duration=1h

# Error: MUTE requires one
/enforce Steve @measure(MUTE)

# Correct
/enforce Steve @measure(MUTE) duration=3h

Severity and commutation

The severity column is not decorative: it classifies every @commute as mitigation (toward lower severity) or aggravation (toward higher), and the two directions carry separate permission nodes.

# CUSTODY(6) -> SUSPENSION(5): mitigation
# needs minutiae.commute.mitigate (staff bundle)
/enforce Steve ::cheating @commute(SUSPENSION) duration=30d

# MUTE(2) -> SUSPENSION(5): aggravation
# needs minutiae.commute.aggravate (admin bundle)
/enforce Steve ::toxic @commute(SUSPENSION) duration=3d

A junior tier can therefore soften configured policy but never harden it - by permission structure, not by convention.

Connection-blocking measures

SUSPENSION and CUSTODY refuse the connection at login with a localised refusal screen. Three things treat them specially:

  • Fingerprints. Connection signals are captured with the sanction and feed evasion detection.
  • Lifting. They require the dedicated minutiae.lift.blocking node to lift; see the target-dependent gates.
  • Shadowing. A ban carrying @shadow admits the player and isolates them on join instead of refusing at the door, so the ban is not revealed by the login screen.

Behavioural constraints and measures

MUTE and QUARANTINE apply their in-session constraint through the same machinery as the behavioural annotations (@ghost, @rubberband, @shadow). Consequences worth knowing:

  • Overlapping constraints from several sanctions merge: the longest expiry per constraint wins, and lifting one sanction removes only its own contribution.
  • Constraints survive relogs: they are recomputed from active sanctions on every join.
  • On multi-server deployments, behavioural state propagates through the directive bus to whichever instance the player is on.

Display names

The identifiers in this page (MUTE, CUSTODY) are command and configuration vocabulary. What players and staff see is the localised display name from the language bundle - "Temporary Ban", "Временный бан" - per recipient. Renaming a measure for your audience is a bundle edit, not a code concern; see Localization.

Choosing a measure

First minor offence Let @warn-first on the layout decide; do not hand-pick WARN.
Needs to sting, no lasting record of effect KICK, or CENSURE when you want precedent without removal.
Chat problemMUTE.
Under investigation right now @remand (quarantine holding action), not a hand-rolled QUARANTINE.
Serious, recoverable SUSPENSION with an escalation ladder ending in permanent.
Beyond recoveryCUSTODY.
