Annotations reference

An annotation is a token of the form @name or @name(parameters) attached to a sanction. This page lists every built-in annotation with its parameters, scope, and permission node.

Syntax

@flag                      no parameters
@name(value)               one positional parameter
@notify(staff, discord)    several positional parameters
@evidence(url="https://example.org/clip", type=video)
!@escalate                 negation: removes a layout-supplied annotation

Values containing commas or spaces are double-quoted. Negation (!@name) is valid only inline on a command and only removes annotations supplied by the selected layout.

Scope

config + inline Usable both in layouts.yml and on a command.
inline only Usable on a command only; rejected in configuration, where it would have no coherent meaning.

Every annotation requires the permission node minutiae.annotation.<name>; the node column is omitted below since it never deviates from this convention. Layout-supplied annotations are permission-checked against the issuing sender too, so a layout cannot smuggle a flag past a junior moderator's grant.

Behavioural flags

These change what the sanction does to the player in session. All are config + inline.

@shadow Covert isolation: the player is hidden from others, their interactions are voided, and their chat is echoed only to themselves. A shadowed ban admits the connection and isolates on join instead of refusing at the door. Implies @silent.
@ghost Hides the player from others and voids their world interactions. Unlike shadow, the player is informed nothing is being concealed from them.
@rubberband Reverts any movement exceeding the configured leash distance per move.
@ip-lock Marks the sanction for address-level enforcement against the captured connection signals.
@silent Suppresses the public broadcast of the sanction.
@transcript Captures and persists the subject's recent chat lines with the sanction. Viewable as CSV in the web panel.

Policy modifiers

These change how the sanction is computed or ages. All are config + inline.

@escalate Engages the layout's escalation ladder against the player's precedent under the cited rule.
@warn-first(n) The first n offences under the rule become formal warnings instead of the measure. @now bypasses the gate.
@decay(30d) The sanction stops counting toward precedent after the window elapses.
@probation(2w) Opens a probation window; a repeat offence inside it is aggravated per the escalation policy in config.yml.
@tariff(1d) A minimum-duration floor applied after escalation. Temporal measures only.
@appeal(on|off) Whether the sanction may be appealed. Defaults to on.
@evidence Evidence requirement or attachment. Forms: bare, @evidence(required), or @evidence(url=..., type=...).
@notify(ch...) Routes a sanction summary to the named notification channels.
@suspended(30d) Records a suspended sentence: inactive until another sanction under the same rule inside the window activates it. Incompatible with @stay.
@expunge(90d) Schedules removal from docket visibility.
@review(7d) Schedules a mandatory staff review; due reviews are claimed once and dispatched to the default channels.

Inline directives

Command-only tokens consumed by the resolver.

@measure(MUTE) Sets the measure of a manual sanction. Mutually exclusive with a layout, which defines its own.
@commute(SUSPENSION) Replaces a layout's measure. Mitigating and aggravating directions carry separate permission nodes.
@count(P.4.1) Cites an additional rule. Repeatable; a manual sanction promotes the first count to its primary rule.
@remand A standalone holding action: quarantine pending a decision. Cannot combine with any measure source.
@reason(text) Sets the reason; equivalent to the reason= override.
@stay(2h) Defers activation for the window; the next sanction against the player activates pending stays. Incompatible with @suspended.
@now Bypasses the warn-first gate: the measure applies immediately regardless of warning quota.
@dry-run Resolves and reports the full outcome without persisting or applying anything.
@as(name) Attributes the sanction to another staff name. Admin tier.
@backdate(2d) Shifts the issue timestamp into the past. Requires @evidence by the default relationship matrix.
@link(ref) Attaches an external reference such as a ticket URL.
@note(text) Attaches an internal case note, never shown to the subject.
@waive(name) Waives a companion requirement imposed by the relationship matrix for this one command.
@amend(id) Modifies an existing sanction (rule, reason, duration, counts) instead of issuing a new one.
@weave(#id) Joins the new sanction under an existing case; chain members share precedent.

The relationship matrix

Annotations relate to each other through three operator-editable relations in config.yml:

annotations:
  implies:
    shadow: [silent]        # applying @shadow also applies @silent
  requires:
    backdate: [evidence]    # @backdate demands @evidence (waivable)
  conflicts: []             # unordered pairs that cannot co-occur
implies Transitive expansion: the implied set is added automatically and shown as (implied) in reports.
requires A hard companion requirement, checked after expansion. @waive(name) lifts it per command for holders of minutiae.waive.
conflicts A symmetric exclusion; the command is rejected naming both offenders.
Warning

An implication chain that produces an internal conflict is reported at boot but not removed; the rejection happens when a command actually assembles the conflicting set. Watch the boot log after editing this section.

Annotations on lifts

The lift command accepts a closed subset plus one lift-only directive: @silent, @dry-run, @note, @notify, @probation, and @cascade. Semantics are covered in Lifting sanctions.
